1. Focus on absurd, surreal, or nightmarish situations that highlight the alienation of modern life.

2. Use a matter-of-fact tone to describe bizarre or unsettling events, as if they were ordinary occurrences.

3. Create a sense of anxiety, futility, or powerlessness in the protagonist's interactions with authority or bureaucracy.

4. Employ symbolism and metaphor to explore themes of guilt, isolation, and existential dread.

5. Write in clear, precise language but leave much unexplained, creating a sense of ambiguity and unease.

6. Start in medias res - drop the reader into the middle of a strange situation without much context.

7. Focus on the psychological state of the protagonist rather than extensive plot or action.

8. Use dark humor or irony to underscore the absurdity of the situations.

9. Create a dreamlike atmosphere where logic and reality seem distorted.

10. End on an unresolved or ambiguous note, leaving the reader with lingering questions.
